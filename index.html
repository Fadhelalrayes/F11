Introduction to Bahrain

Bahrain, known as the "Pearl of the Gulf," is a small island nation in the Arabian Gulf renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and modern attractions. With a blend of ancient heritage and contemporary luxury, Bahrain offers something for every traveler. From pristine beaches and world-class shopping to historical sites and delicious cuisine, this guide will help you explore the best of Bahrain. 4

As of 2025, Bahrain continues to attract tourists with its flourishing hospitality sector and events like the Formula 1 Grand Prix. 2

History of Bahrain

Bahrain's history dates back over 5,000 years, making it one of the oldest civilizations in the world. It was home to the Dilmun civilization, known for its trade and pearl diving. The island has been influenced by various empires, including the Persians, Portuguese, and British. 6

Key historical sites include the Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain), a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the ancient burial mounds that dot the landscape. 0

Culture and Traditions

Bahrain's culture is a fusion of Arab traditions with modern influences. Islam is the predominant religion, and you'll find beautiful mosques throughout the country. The people are known for their hospitality, often inviting visitors for tea or meals. 5

Celebrate festivals like Eid and National Day, or explore souqs (markets) for traditional crafts, spices, and gold. Bahraini music, dance, and poetry are integral to its cultural scene. 1

Bahraini Cuisine

Bahrain's food scene is diverse, influenced by Arab, Persian, and Indian flavors. Must-try dishes include:

  • Machboos: Spiced rice with meat or fish.
  • Halwa: A sweet treat made from sugar, nuts, and rosewater.
  • Seafood: Fresh from the Gulf, like grilled hammour.
  • Shawarma and falafel for quick bites.

Don't miss street food in souqs or fine dining in luxury hotels. 3

Accommodation Options

Bahrain offers a range of stays from budget to luxury:

  • Luxury: The Ritz-Carlton, Four Seasons Bahrain Bay.
  • Mid-range: Wyndham Garden Manama, Novotel Bahrain Al Dana Resort.
  • Budget: Hostels and guesthouses in Manama.

Many hotels are beachfront with spas and pools. 8

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: October to April for mild weather. 6
  • Visa: Many nationalities get visa on arrival or eVisa. 3
  • Currency: Bahraini Dinar (BHD). Credit cards widely accepted.
  • Transportation: Taxis, buses, or rent a car. Bahrain is small and easy to navigate.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly in public, especially at religious sites. Respect local customs during Ramadan.
  • Safety: Bahrain is safe for tourists, but stay aware in crowded areas. 1
