Why Buy from the USA?

The US market offers a huge selection of vehicles at competitive prices, including auction cars, dealer stock, and rare models. Buyers worldwide often save money even after adding shipping and customs fees.

Step-by-Step Process

1. Search & Select

Choose your vehicle through trusted marketplaces. Check VIN history using Carfax or AutoCheck.

2. Inspection

Use third-party inspection services to confirm the car’s condition before bidding or buying.

3. Payment

Prefer secure methods: bank wire or escrow services. Avoid direct cash/wallet transfers.

4. Shipping

Choose RoRo (cheaper, for running cars) or Container (safer, for high-value or non-running cars).

5. Customs Clearance

Hire a local customs broker in your country to handle import duties, VAT, and registration paperwork.

Shipping & Costs

Average shipping costs range from $900 to $2,500 depending on port, method, and vehicle size.

Common Ports
  • East Coast: New York, Baltimore, Miami
  • West Coast: Los Angeles, Oakland
  • Gulf: Houston
Methods
  • RoRo (Roll-on/Roll-off): Cheaper, for running cars
  • Container: More secure, can ship multiple cars or with spare parts
Extra Costs
  • Export documentation: $100–$300
  • Insurance during shipping: ~1–2% of car value
  • Destination port & customs clearance fees

Tips & Risks

  • Always check VIN history before purchase.
  • Use escrow services to avoid scams.
  • Avoid unrealistically low prices.
  • Work with licensed shipping and customs brokers only.
